ASTRO PHOTOGRAPHY

Abell

Abell 2065 - The Corona Borealis Galaxy Cluster. The cluster lies at a distance of 1 billion light-years according to Hubble in the constellation Corona Borealis, the Northern Crown. To give you an indication of the speed of light, 1 light second equals roughly the distance from San Francisco to new York and back, 30 times a second. I can't fathom the enormous distance light travels in in one billion years.

California Nebula

The California Nebula is a huge cloud of hydrogen gas called an H-II region. It is located 2000 light-years from us. The gas is excited by the bright star in the picture. Visually this emission nebula is very difficult to observe, even with large telescopes, because it's dim light is spread over an area four times the diameter of the full moon. The picture composed of 75-5 minute exposure, over several nights.

IC 5070

IC 5070 - The Pelican Nebula is part of a huge H-II cloud of hydrogen gas, energized by nearby luminous stars and then emits its own light at the red wavelength of hydrogen. It is located at a distance of 1500 light-years in the constellation Cygnus the Swan, in our own galaxy, the Milky Way.
